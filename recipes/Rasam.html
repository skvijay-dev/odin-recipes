Rasam

About Rasam

Rasam is a warm, spicy, and tangy South Indian soup made with tamarind, tomatoes, black pepper, and aromatic spices. It is usually served with steamed rice or can be enjoyed as a light, comforting soup on its own. Rasam is known for its bold flavors and is often used as a soothing dish when you're feeling under the weather.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium tomato (chopped)
  • 1 small lemon-sized tamarind piece (or 1 tbsp tamarind paste)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 cloves garlic (crushed)
  • Â½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • A few curry leaves (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Make the base: Soak tamarind in warm water and extract the juice. Mix with chopped tomatoes, turmeric, pepper, cumin, garlic, salt, and water.
  2. Boil: Bring the mixture to a gentle boil until it smells aromatic.
  3. Temper: In a small pan, heat oil/ghee. Add mustard seeds and curry leaves until they splutter. Pour this over the rasam.
  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or enjoy as a soup.
