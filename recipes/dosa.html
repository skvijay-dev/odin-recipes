Dosa

About Dosa

Dosa is a thin, crispy pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and lentils. It is a popular South Indian dish, usually served with coconut chutney and sambar (a spicy lentil soup). Dosa is known for its light, crispy texture and slightly tangy flavor. It’s often enjoyed as a breakfast or a light meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • ½ cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Steps

  1. Soak: Soak rice and urad dal separately for at least 4 hours.
  2. Grind: Grind both into a smooth batter. Add salt and let it ferment overnight.
  3. Cook: Pour a ladle of batter on a hot pan and spread it thin. Drizzle a little oil around the edges.
  4. Serve: Cook until golden and crispy. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.
