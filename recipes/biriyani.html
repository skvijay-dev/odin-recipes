Biriyani

About biriyani

Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish that originated in the Indian subcontinent. It is made by layering fragrant basmati rice with tender pieces of marinated meat, such as chicken, beef, or mutton, and slow-cooking them together with a blend of rich spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves. Often garnished with fried onions, fresh herbs, and boiled eggs, biryani is a festive dish that is loved for its bold taste and delicious, spicy aroma.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 300g chicken pieces
  • 1 large onion (sliced)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon biryani masala

Steps

  1. Cook the rice: Boil the basmati rice until it's about 80% cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan, fry sliced onions until golden. Add chicken, tomatoes, yogurt, salt, and all spices. Cook until the chicken is done and the sauce thickens.
  3. Layer the biryani: In a pot, layer the cooked chicken and rice. Top with coriander leaves.
  4. Steam: Cover and cook on low heat for about 10 minutes to let the flavors mix.
  5. Serve: Fluff gently and serve hot.
